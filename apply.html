Expand your skills at our free, intensive learn-to-code bootcamp

Your future is on the Web

CodeSquad is holding a series of Coding & Career Events to introduce you to our program. Are you curious about software? Have you ever experimented with HTML or JavaScript, or taken an online course? We can help you learn more. Our curriculum includes training in the "full stack" of web development, from HTML and CSS to server-side JavaScript, Node, Express and more, as well as soft skills in effective communication, Agile methodology, and job readiness.

All events are held at Lena Park Community Development, 150 American Legion Highway in Dorchester. To apply to our program, you must have a high school diploma or GED, and be legal to work in the United States through December 2020. However, NO coding experience is required, and there is NO maximum age. Classes are tentatively scheduled for Monday, Tuesday and Wednesday evenings, 6:30pm-9pm, and Saturdays 9am-2pm. Classes will run from Monday, January 7 to Saturday, June 15, 2019.

This is a great time to learn web development. A July 2017 Skill-Works report found that in Greater Boston, for every 17 job openings in IT, there was only ONE qualified applicant. Not just technology companies, but health care providers, manufacturers and financial services firms are all desperate for more programmers. At CodeSquad, we believe this presents an opportunity. And we want you to be part of it.

CodeSquad is a nonprofit organization that trains talented adults from low-income families to become full-stack developers and launch a new career.
